HIS MAJESTY JIGME kHESAR NAMGYEL WANGCHUCK

About his majesty

Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck is the current King of Bhutan. Born on February 21, 1980, he ascended to the throne on December 9, 2006, after his father, King Jigme Singye Wangchuck, abdicated in his favor. Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck is the fifth king of the Wangchuck dynasty, which has ruled Bhutan since 1907.

His Majesty Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck is widely respected and revered by the people of Bhutan. He has played a significant role in promoting and preserving Bhutan's unique cultural heritage and fostering the country's development in a balanced and sustainable manner. Bhutan is known for its unique approach to measuring Gross National Happiness (GNH) as an indicator of the country's progress, and King Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck has supported initiatives to enhance the well-being of the Bhutanese people.

Additionally, King Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck has been involved in various diplomatic activities, representing Bhutan on the global stage. He has been recognized for his efforts in strengthening international relations and promoting Bhutan's values of peace, happiness, and sustainable development.
